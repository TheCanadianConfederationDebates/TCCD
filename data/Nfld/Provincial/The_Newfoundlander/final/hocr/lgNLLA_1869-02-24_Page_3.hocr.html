ST. JOHN'S : W.E.D.NESDAY, FEBRUARY 24, 1869.

THE Government propositions as to the terms on which they think this colony should enter into Con­federation were introduced for discussion in Com­mittee of the Assembly yesterday by the Hon. At­torney General; Mr. Glen, the hon. Mr. Shea, and Mr. Hogett followed, when the Committee rose and the debate was a journed to to-day. These terms (for which we have not room in cr­tenuo to-day), are in the first place that the Domi­mion Government shall defray a sum of $208,462 of our public expenditure, being the salaries and ex­penses of thoso departments of which they would assume control, Governor, Judges, Customs, Post Office, Light Houses, Surveyor Goneral, &c. &c.

In addition to this sum of $20S,462 would be Assets applicable to local pur­poses $408,922

“Together with...the just claims of this colony for other charges and - of like charact with those borne or made by the Dominion with reference to the other provinces.”

The public debt and liabilities to be assumed by the Dominion Government. No tax to be levied on our exports unless a similar one be laid on all the staple products of the other provinces.

Domition Government to assume the charge for interest on £50,000 of Water Company's Stock. Oceanic, Colonial and Coastal Steam Communica­tion to be provided by the Government of the Dominion. No final arrangement until an appeal be made to the people at next gennral election. In connection with these proposed terms, is a plan of a future constitution on a reduced scale, abolishing the Legislative Council, reducing the number of representatives to fifteen, and largely decreasing allowances for salaries &c. Tais would bring the future expenditure for these services down to $245,810, which being defrayed from the above stated sum applicable to local purposes, viz., $408,922, would leave an annual balance available for roads and other public improvements of abou t $163,112. We think the opinion of the country will infallibly be that these terms comprise a most equitable arrangement, and that the colony should not hesitate to ratily it. -
