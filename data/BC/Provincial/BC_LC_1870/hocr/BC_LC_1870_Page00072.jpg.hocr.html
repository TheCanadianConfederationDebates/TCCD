72 CONFEDERATION DEBATE.

Hon. MK. HELMCKEN—Would it be better to have Confederation with no terms at all, or with terms equal to these?

Hon. MR. \VOOD-—I ask for different terms; power to impose our own tariff, for instance.

Hon. Du. HELMCKEN—The objections of the Hon. Member are not correct. When the Dock and Railway are built, I admit that some source of labour, and consequent interest, will be gone, but then we must look for other interests to arise. I admit that discontent may arise; it has frequently been so in England, when a large number of labourers have been thrown out of employment; but I say that the Dominion and Local Governments will have the power and the sense to remedy any such evils as they occur.

Hon. MR, PEMBIGR’i‘ON—I would ask the Chief Commissioner of Lands and Works whether it would be possible to connect the supply of water with the Docks?
