Under the Canadian law a brewer must take out a brewer’s and maltster’s license, and has to pay one cent per pound on all malt made, and as there is an average amount of 1,248,000 pounds of malt consumed in the year, the average duty would amount to $12,680 per annum, in addition to which they will have to pay a maltster’s and brewer’s license. The duty upon that amount of malt now is'$3,750. Confederation therefore will increase the malt duty by nearly $9.000. Brewers would probably buy all their mail: from abroad or cease to brew, especially when we take into consideration the annoyances connected with the bonding system. You will see, Sir, that this quantity of malt would take 500 acres of land to raise it, so that in addition to injuring the brewers, the farmers are also injured. .

Under the Canadian Law, salmon must not be taken at the mouth of any river when they are going up for the purpose of spawning We all know that they must be taken. If we are not allowed to catch them as they go up, we should never get them at all. They never come down again; they go up to die.

Again, according to Canadian Law, whales must not be taken by means of bombs or ﬁre arms: and I am told they cannot be taken without firearms in these waters, so that under Confederation whales would be free to spout as they pleased.
