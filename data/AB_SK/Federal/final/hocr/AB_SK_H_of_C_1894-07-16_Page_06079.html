NORTH-WEST TERRITORIES ACT.

Sir JOHN THOMPSON moved third read- ing of Bill (No. 149) further to amend the Act. respecting the North-west Territories.

Mr. McCARTHY. Mr. Speaker. I do not rise to object to anything the Bill contains, but, for the purpose of asking the House to add to the measure an amendment which, I think, on reflection and consideration, the House will believe to be in the public in terest. In 1891 we conferred upon the Leg- islative Assembly of the North-west Terri- tories almost complete power, power almost as full as that which the provincial bodies enjoy under the scheme of the British North America Act, by which a certain portion of legislative authority is vested in the provinces, while a certain portion of author- ity is retained by this Parliament. But we did not confer on them any additional au- thority to that they already enjoyed in the matter of education, and it is in regard to that subject I propose to draw the attention of the House this evening. It cannot too firmly be kept in mind that, according to the scheme of the distribution of legislative authority, the subject of education was de- clared to be a matter of local concern, and, therefore, properly belonging to the pro- vince; and my argument will be that, as we have conferred upon the Legislative Assembly of‘ the North-west Territories nearly all the power and authority which have been conferred upon the provinces, this power or right to deal with the matter of education should not be withheld. Let me, for a moment. draw the attention of the House to the provisions of the North- west Territories Act of 1891. By that Act it was determined that the Legislative As- sembly “ shall, subject to the provisions of this Act, and of any other Act of the Par- liament of Canada at any time in force in the Territories, have power to make ordi- nances for the government of the Territories in relation to the class of subject next here- unto mentioned, that is to say."
