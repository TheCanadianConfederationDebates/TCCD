Mr. Mills asked if the Government intended to ask the Imperial confirmation of the power of this Bill, or whether it would be a tentative one, which might be withdrawn?

Hon. Sir John A. Macdonald said there was some doubt in this respect about the appoint- ment of Senators; with regard to other portions of the Bill, the course to be adopted will depend a great deal on the way in which the Bill passed in Parliament.

Hon. Mr. Wood thought with the exception of Senators the provisions of the Bill would be embraced under an Imperial Order in Council.

Hon. Sir John A. Macdonald said the ques- tion would be open for a further consideration.

The debate was then adjourned.
