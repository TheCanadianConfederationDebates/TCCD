I am not one of those who ever had the war- fever; I have not believed in getting up large armaments in this country; I have never doubted that a military spirit, to a certain ex- tent, did necessarily form part of the character of a great people ; but I felt that Canada had not yet reached that stage in her progress when she could safely assume the duty of de- fence; and that, so long as peace continued and the Mother Country threw her shield around us, it was well for us to cultivate our ﬁelds and grow in numbers and material strength, until we could look our enemies fearlessly in the face.
