THE PROROGATION

Honourable Gentlemen of the Senate:— men of the House of Commons:—

The time has come when I am able to re- lease you from your labours, and I have the greatest pleasure in doing so, inasmuch as the session of which the opening was delayed for the reasons you are cognizant of, has ne- cessarily been protracted to a period some- what later than suits the general conveni- ence. Satisfaction may, I think, be express- ed at the result of your deliberations. In addition to the long list of measures and amendments on subjects of internal policy which has passed under your notice, your records show that memorable steps have been taken towards the accomplishment of the great scheme of uniting the whole of British North America in a single Confederation.
